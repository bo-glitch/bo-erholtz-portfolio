About

I like the point where research starts becoming a real decision.

I am a senior at the Carlson School of Management studying Marketing, Business Analytics, and Entrepreneurial Management. Across internships, class projects, and venture work, I have learned that I am most interested in the work that happens before a recommendation is obvious: digging through data, noticing patterns, asking better questions, and building a clearer path forward.

My work tends to sit between strategy and execution. I like research that turns into a campaign, analysis that changes a priority list, and presentations that make a messy problem easier for other people to understand. I am building a skill set around curiosity, structure, and useful business judgment.

Pattern Finder

I connect signals across competitors, customers, markets, and internal data to understand what is really driving a problem.

Structure Builder

I turn broad questions into frameworks, models, and next steps that make decisions easier to act on.

Business Storyteller

I care about making work understandable, not just complete. The best analysis still needs a clear story.
