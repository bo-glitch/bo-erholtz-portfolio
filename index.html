About

I am drawn to the work before the answer looks obvious.

I am a senior at the Carlson School of Management, graduating in May 2026 through the University Honors Program. I have taken the long route on purpose: Marketing taught me how people choose, Business Analytics taught me how to interrogate data, and Entrepreneurial Management taught me how ideas become operating plans.

The through-line is curiosity with a practical edge. At Daikin, that meant cleaning up messy market inputs and turning them into recruiting recommendations. In class projects, it has meant studying shopper trust, LLM adoption, employee engagement, and business model sustainability. I like when the question is still fuzzy and the work is to make it sharper.

Comfortable With Imperfect Inputs

I have worked with labor data, pricing files, survey responses, interviews, competitor research, and internal systems where the first job is making the information usable.

Turning Questions Into Direction

I build scoring models, frameworks, timelines, and recommendation decks that help teams compare options and move from open-ended questions to clearer next steps.

Clear Communication Matters

I care about making analysis usable for people who were not in the spreadsheet, the interview, or the class discussion. The recommendation has to travel well.
