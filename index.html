About

I’m a strategy and analytics-focused business student who likes turning ambiguity into direction.

I’m graduating soon from the University of Minnesota Carlson School of Management, where I am triple majoring in Marketing, Business Analytics, and Entrepreneurial Management. Across internships, consulting-style projects, and venture coursework, I’ve learned that I’m most energized by work that starts with a messy question and ends with a clear recommendation.

My experience spans market intelligence, digital strategy, workforce analysis, consumer research, business modeling, and early-stage venture planning. I’m especially interested in roles across strategy, analytics, growth, insights, and business operations where I can connect research, data, and decision-making.

Pattern finder

I like comparing signals across sources, whether that means competitor messaging, labor markets, customer behavior, or campaign data.

Builder of structure

I turn broad questions into frameworks, models, timelines, and recommendations that make the next decision easier.

Business storyteller

I care about making analysis usable through clear writing, visuals, and presentations that connect the insight to the decision.
